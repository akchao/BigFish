ABOUT THE PROJECT

Key features of our project and the queries we implemented

Queries 1 & 2

What are the patents owned by a child company (aka. acquired) or a parent company? What categories do these companies typically file patents in - Which parent companies are direct friend’s (competitors) one-hop-away of the selected parent company?

Queries 3 & 4

How has a particular company's patent filing categories changed over time? How many of the acquired companies are located near the parent company?

Queries 5 & 6

what percentage of parents’ and children’s patents fall in each category (CPC subgroup title)? In which years did the most acquisitions happen? How many acquisitions did each company make within the last 20 years?

Queries 7 & 8

Which classes of patents do these companies consistently renew? What are the top 5 classes of patents that had the most renewals? What are all the patents that fall in a layman category (or technology) which the parent company owns?

Project Inspiration

Tech giants like Google, Amazon, Facebook and Apple have a number of strategic reasons for acquiring companies—to acquire talent, to thwart competitors, or simply to gain access to those company’s patents to diversify their product offerings or to set up the capability to expand into a particular area. It is not always clear why these companies move in a particular direction, for instance Google moving into healthcare or Amazon choosing food distribution.

For our project, we wanted to create a tool that connects, explores, and visualizes company acquisitions, patent filings, and patent assignment transfers to see what it can reveal about how these tech giants have segmented the market over time as well as anticipate how they might evolve over time based on the patents they hold.
